“The oceans play a critical role in regulating Earth's climate. They act as a giant heat sink, absorbing a whopping 90% of the excess heat trapped by greenhouse gasses. Additionally, oceans take in around 25% of the carbon dioxide we release, acting as a vital carbon sink and slowing the pace of warming.

However, this buffering capacity isn't limitless. As the climate continues to change, these vital services are under threat. This short introduction is just the beginning – dive deeper to explore the complex ways our oceans are both impacted by and influence climate change.”
